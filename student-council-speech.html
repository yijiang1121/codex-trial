Student Council Speech: Original & Enhancements

Review the original student council campaign speech and see how targeted revisions can boost energy, clarity, and audience connection.

Original Speech

Hello! I’m running to be one of your student council representatives.

I’m doing this for two main reasons. First, I want to make school lunch better. A lot of students have been complaining about the food. Second, I want to bring more fun to recess!

Let me start with what I plan to do about school lunch. I’ll work on bringing more variety to the menu. Sometimes, the only option is a corn dog, and I think it would be great to have more choices — like different vegetables and protein options.

I also want to improve the quality of the food. One time, a friend and I got yogurt that was expired. That shouldn’t happen. I believe it’s important for us to have healthy, high-quality meals.

Now, about recess — I want to make it more fun by making sure there’s enough equipment for everyone. For example, we only have two bouncy balls at school, and a lot of kids want to use them but don’t get a turn.

Thanks for listening! If you like my ideas, please vote for me!

Engaging Revision Highlights

Each revision keeps the original meaning while adding vivid detail, audience awareness, or a call to action. Highlighted phrases show the new wording that adds energy or specificity.

Opening Greeting

Original

Hello! I’m running to be one of your student council representatives.

Revised

Hello, Falcons! I’m excited to run as one of your student council representatives and be a stronger voice for our grade.

Why I’m Running

Original

I’m doing this for two main reasons. First, I want to make school lunch better. A lot of students have been complaining about the food. Second, I want to bring more fun to recess!

Revised

I’m running for two big reasons. First, I want to make school lunch better because so many of you have told me the food feels repetitive. Second, I want to turn recess into the highlight of our day with more games and shared fun!

Lunch Variety Plan

Original

Let me start with what I plan to do about school lunch. I’ll work on bringing more variety to the menu. Sometimes, the only option is a corn dog, and I think it would be great to have more choices — like different vegetables and protein options.

Revised

Let’s start with lunch. I’ll work with our cafeteria team to bring more variety to the menu. Instead of just another corn dog, imagine picking from fresh veggie sides, new proteins, and seasonal specials.

Food Quality Assurance

Original

I also want to improve the quality of the food. One time, a friend and I got yogurt that was expired. That shouldn’t happen. I believe it’s important for us to have healthy, high-quality meals.

Revised

Quality matters too. A friend and I once opened expired yogurt—that shouldn’t happen. I’ll push for regular freshness checks so every meal is safe, healthy, and something we’re proud to eat.

Recess Improvements

Original

Now, about recess — I want to make it more fun by making sure there’s enough equipment for everyone. For example, we only have two bouncy balls at school, and a lot of kids want to use them but don’t get a turn.

Revised

For recess, I want to make sure fun never runs out. Right now we only have two bouncy balls, so lots of friends miss out. I’ll organize an equipment wishlist and rotating game stations so everyone gets a turn.

Closing Call to Action

Original

Thanks for listening! If you like my ideas, please vote for me!

Revised

Thanks for listening! If these ideas sound exciting, I’d be honored to earn your vote and work hard for you.
